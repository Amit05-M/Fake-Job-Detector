10 Red Flags That Instantly Identify a Fake Job Post in India

April 2026 · 5 min read

Job fraud in India has reached alarming levels. Whether it's a WhatsApp message, a Naukri listing, or an email from a "recruiter," scammers have become increasingly sophisticated at making fake job offers look real. Here are 10 warning signs you should always check before responding to any job post.

1. Asks for a registration fee or security deposit

This is the single biggest red flag. No legitimate employer ever asks candidates to pay money — whether it's called a "registration fee," "training cost," "security deposit," or "ID verification charge." If a job post asks for any payment from the applicant, it is almost certainly a scam.

2. Contact only via WhatsApp or Telegram

Professional recruiters use corporate email addresses, LinkedIn InMail, or official company portals. A recruiter who insists on communicating only via WhatsApp or Telegram and avoids giving you an official company email is a major warning sign.

3. Unrealistic salary for the role

Posts offering ₹50,000 per week, ₹1 lakh per month for freshers, or any salary significantly above the market rate for that role and experience level are almost always fake. Scammers use inflated salaries to attract desperate job seekers.

4. Generic or vague job description

Real job posts have detailed role descriptions, specific required skills, and clear responsibilities. Fake posts are often vague: "data entry work," "online part-time job," or "earn from home" with no specific job title or skill requirements.

5. Gmail or Yahoo recruiter email

If you get an offer letter or interview call from hr.infosys@gmail.com or tcs.recruitment@yahoo.com, it is fake. All legitimate companies use their own domain for recruitment communications.

📱 WhatsApp Scams

WhatsApp Job Scams in India: How They Work and How to Stay Safe

March 2026 · 6 min read

WhatsApp has become the preferred channel for job scammers in India. With over 500 million Indian users on WhatsApp, it's a massive platform for fraudsters to reach potential victims. Understanding how these scams operate is the first step to protecting yourself.

How WhatsApp job scams typically operate

The typical scam follows a predictable pattern. First, you receive an unsolicited WhatsApp message from an unknown number. The message introduces the sender as an HR manager or recruiter from a well-known company — often Infosys, TCS, Amazon, Flipkart, or a foreign company. The message includes a job offer that sounds attractive: good salary, flexible hours, work from home option.

If you respond with interest, they send you a formal-looking "offer letter" as a PDF. The design often mimics the actual company branding. The letter then asks you to pay a "security deposit" or "registration fee" to "activate your employment." Once you pay, the scammer disappears.

Variations of the WhatsApp job scam

  • Task-based scams: You're asked to complete simple tasks (liking videos, writing reviews) for payment, then asked to invest money to "unlock" larger payouts
  • Data harvesting: The "application" requires your Aadhaar, PAN, and bank details for "background verification"
  • Fake interview calls: You receive a WhatsApp call posing as an HR interview, followed by a fake offer letter and fee demand
⚠️ Golden rule: If you didn't apply for a job on an official platform, and someone contacts you out of nowhere on WhatsApp with an offer — assume it is a scam until proven otherwise.

How to verify a WhatsApp job offer

Always visit the official company website directly and search for the job opening in their official careers section. Call the company's official customer service number (from their website, not the number provided in the message) and ask if the recruiter is genuine. Never pay any fee of any kind.

🏛️ Government Job Scams

Fake Government Job Scams in India: UPSC, Railways, SSC Fraud Explained

February 2026 · 5 min read

Government jobs are among the most sought-after positions in India, offering job security, good salaries, and social status. Fraudsters exploit this demand by creating elaborate fake government job offers. These scams are particularly dangerous because they often look very official and convincing.

Common fake government job scams

Scammers create fake websites that look like official government portals for UPSC, SSC, Railways (RRB/RRC), state PSCs, banking (IBPS, SBI), and defence recruitment. They post fake job notifications with realistic-looking advertisement numbers, eligibility criteria, and application deadlines.

The scam unfolds in stages: first a small "application fee" (₹500–2000), then an "exam fee," then a "joining fee" or "medical test fee," and finally a "training deposit." Victims may pay multiple times before realizing the scam.

Key facts about genuine government jobs

  • All genuine central government job applications are on official .gov.in domains
  • Application fees for government jobs are fixed and paid only through official payment gateways
  • No government recruiter will ever contact you on WhatsApp about an unsolicited offer
  • Offer letters for government jobs are issued only after the complete selection process — not before an interview
  • SC/ST/PWD candidates are usually exempt from application fees — beware if even they're asked to pay

Official portals to check for real government jobs

Always check: upsc.gov.in for UPSC, ssc.nic.in for SSC, indianrailways.gov.in for Railways, ibps.in for banking, and respective state government .gov.in websites for state jobs. If a job isn't listed on these official portals, it is not real.

💻 WFH Scams

Work From Home Job Scams in India: How to Tell Real from Fake

January 2026 · 4 min read

Since the pandemic, work-from-home (WFH) jobs have become mainstream — and so have WFH job scams. These scams target people looking for flexible work, housewives, students, and recently unemployed individuals. The WFH scam category is one of the most reported types of job fraud in India.

How to recognize a fake WFH job post

  • Vague role description: "Online work," "part-time home job," "data entry" with no specifics
  • No company name: Legitimate WFH employers are real companies you can look up
  • Hourly rates too high: ₹500–1000/hour for simple tasks with no required skills is a fantasy
  • Starter kit or software fee: Any request to purchase a "starter kit" or download paid software to start work is a scam
  • Direct bank transfer payments: Real employers pay via salary through official banking channels, not via PhonePe or Google Pay with conditions attached
🚩 A real remote employer will pay you — not ask you to pay them first. If someone asks you for money to "activate" your work-from-home job, stop immediately.

Genuine WFH jobs in India come from real companies on platforms like LinkedIn, Indeed, Naukri, or Internshala.
